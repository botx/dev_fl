Directions for Development of Payment Systems Under the New Financial Sector Landscape of Thailand

Following the COVID-19 pandemic, global society has rapidly evolved into the digital age and has become a period of transition that requires adaptation to new lifestyles. Digital trends have influenced the global financial system, and the financial sector is among those in greatest need of adjustment. The financial system in Thailand, too, needs to undergo a significant transformation to fully support the transition into a digital society and economy once the COVID-19 crisis is over. The Bank of Thailand (BOT) has issued a policy direction, "Repositioning Thailand's Financial Sector for a sustainable digital economy,” outlining the intention to maintain a proper balance between supporting innovation development, managing risk, and promoting the financial sector resilience. The aim is to enable the financial system to adapt promptly to rapid changes.

Under the new landscape, e-Payment is a key engine driving the financial transformation into a digital economy and sustainable development. Past successes in driving the adoption of e-Payment services have primarily contributed to more connected and flexible economic transactions, innovation developments, and domestic and cross-border payment services that are efficient in terms of convenience, speed, security, and reasonable cost.

The next development phase will further modernize payment systems and efficiently connect service providers, businesses, governments, and the general public through infrastructures and e-Payment services that promote accessibility and foster competition and innovation. Such developments will provide the public with more comprehensive services and applications. At the same time, the business sector is supported with payment infrastructures that accommodate a fully integrated digital business, under flexible, equitable, and effective governance, without posing systemic risk.

The BOT has issued this Payment Directional paper on "Directions for Development of Payment Systems under the New Financial Sector Landscape of Thailand" to guide all relevant sectors in the development of payment systems in the three years (2022–2024) under the principles of Openness, Inclusivity, and Resiliency. The goals are to expand the public adoption of digital payments by 2.5 times to 800 times per person per year while reducing the use of cash and cheques. These goals follow the vision of the payment systems policy, stipulating that "Digital Payments would be the preferred choice among all user groups and would also improve the quality of life, enhance competitiveness and transition Thailand to a ‘less-cash’ society." Such achievement would require the collective force and support of all parties for the Thai financial sector to enter the digital economy sustainably.
