Inclusivity

Promote accessibility and digital financial literacy

Principles

Promote digital payments as the preferred option in all sectors by developing a wide range of services to address users’ needs, motivating usage, providing basic knowledge of how to use digital payment services securely and with understanding, and appropriately assisting those not ready for digital payments during the transition.

Origin of development: Despite the exponential growth in digital payments, there remains room for expansion, and providing an understanding of safe usage is one critical success factor.

  • Thailand's digital payment utilization has increased fivefold in the last five years, from 63 transactions per person per year in 2017 to 312 transactions per person per year in 2021, owing to the development of payment infrastructures and excellent services. Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic and the government's public relief measures have accelerated the widespread adoption of digital payments.
  • Despite the rapid growth of digital payments, current usage is not evenly distributed across all user groups, representing an opportunity to broaden digital payment adoption. According to the Payment Diary survey, although people have begun adjusting their payment behaviors and become accustomed to digital payments, more attention is required to encourage continued use.
  • Promoting digital payments as the preferred payment option requires enhanced knowledge and understanding of digital finance and safe usage, in addition to appropriate assistance for those not yet ready for digital payments.
Principles
  • What we wish to see Increased digital payment usage in the public, business, and government sectors signifies an entrance into a less-cash society. Service providers and governments work together to expand services, promote usage, raise awareness, and create incentives to achieve widespread adoption of digital payments in everyday life. The public has sufficient knowledge and understanding of digital payments to use them correctly and safely and is aware of innovations and financial threats.
  • What we do not wish to see Cash and cheque usage continue to increase. The public lacks the knowledge and understanding of digital payment, resulting in incorrect or improper use, being victimized by scammers, or committing fraud, all of which have ramifications for society as a whole.
