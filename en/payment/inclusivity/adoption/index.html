1. Adoption

Expand and Promote Digital Payments for Improved Inclusiveness

The General Public​

The BOT, in collaboration with financial service providers and the government sector, promotes the use of digital payments on the demand side by creating greater needs for services and on the supply side by developing services, fostering competition, and reducing barriers to digital payment usage. Efforts to shift public payment behaviors toward digital payments and maintain momentum for current utilization include:

  1. Demand Side: Increase the use of digital payments among the active users, early adopters, conservatives, and laggards through the following:

    • Develop payment services that address and adapt to the needs of each user group.
    • Educate and incentivize by encouraging financial service providers to employ more specific strategies to reach each target group as increases in users' confidence can induce repeat usage, leading to familiarity and, eventually, a shift in their behaviors towards digital payments.

  2. Supply Side: Develop new services, promote competition, and reduce barriers to digital payment usage through the following:

    • Encourage the development of new services in applications that provide digital payment services such as e-commerce and mobile commerce to add convenience, provide seamless services, and help maintain and increase the use of digital payment services continually.
    • Extend digital payment services such as PromptPay, Thai QR Code, and government applications based on interoperability principles to connect with a broader range of service providers and digital payment services to reach a larger number of users.
    • Promote continued digital payment usage arising from government projects to expand public adoption.
    • Introduce digital payment services to public transportation systems by collaborating with the Office of Transport and Traffic Policy and Planning (OTP) to develop digital fare payment services that simplify people's daily lives and increase service efficiency.
    • Increase digital payment terminals in distant areas by encouraging acquirers to provide payment terminals for remote merchants or businesses.
    • Decrease service initialization limitations such as mobile data network access by collaborating with telecommunication service providers to broaden public access to mobile banking or e-Wallet and reviewing regulations on user identification and authentication (KYC) to be commensurate with the risks incurred in the transactions.

The Business Sector​

  • Increase adoption of digital payments and digital work processes in the business sector via the PromptBiz system by promoting widespread use through cooperation with the financial and government sectors such as the Comptroller General's Department, the Office of Small and Medium Enterprises Promotion (OSMEP), and the business sector such as the Chamber of Commerce of Thailand, the Federation of Thai Industries and various associations of entrepreneurs.
  • Support the enhancement of the ERP system to enable connection with digital payments to accommodate SMEs by collaborating with local software developers.
  • Raise the digital payment transfer limit to accommodate business transactions requiring higher-value payment and remittance balances.

Government Services​

  • Reduce reliance on cheques in corporate tax payments by partnering with the Revenue Department to expand digital payment adoption and streamline relevant processes correspondingly, particularly for those who already use e-filing for tax returns.
  • Intensify digital payment in public sector payments and adjust the relevant processes for practicality and flexibility by consulting with relevant agencies.
PromptPay's Path to Digital Payment Expansion

PromptPay service is a result of a collaboration between government agencies, the private sector, financial institutions, and the BOT under the National e-Payment project since 2015 to develop services that facilitate funds transfer and e-Payments that are "cheap, easy, convenient, and secure." The service accommodates payment needs in the public, business, and government sectors, such as individual funds transfers, government welfare payments, cross-bank bill payments, and payment reminders (PayAlert). PromptPay has reached 69 million registered numbers, with an average of over 36 million transactions per day and a daily average value of more than 100 billion baht. In addition, the PromptPay service has been extended to support standard QR codes for payments, which are widely available in retail stores, stalls, shopping malls, and public transport services. Currently, there are over 7 million QR payment acceptance points. Digital payment services have helped improve the public's quality of life and access to financial services, which must be accompanied by education on correct and safe usage to foster the widespread adoption of digital payments.
